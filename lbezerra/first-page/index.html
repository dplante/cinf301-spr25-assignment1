History

Periphery is an American progressive metal band formed in Washington, D.C., in 2005 by guitarist Misha Mansoor. They are recognized as pioneers of the "djent" movement within progressive metal. The band's lineup includes vocalist Spencer Sotelo, guitarists Misha Mansoor, Jake Bowen, Mark Holcomb, and drummer Matt Halpern.

Band History:

  • Formation and Early Years (2005–2009): Misha Mansoor initially started Periphery as a solo project, gaining attention through online platforms by sharing his compositions. Over time, the project evolved into a full band with various lineup changes, solidifying its presence in the progressive metal scene.
  • Debut Album and Rising Popularity (2010–2011): In 2010, Periphery released their self-titled debut album, which debuted at No. 128 on the Billboard Top 200 and No. 2 on the Billboard Heatseekers chart. This release marked their entry into the mainstream metal scene.
  • Releases and Evolution (2012–Present): The band continued to evolve their sound with subsequent albums, including "Periphery II: This Time It's Personal" (2012), the dual albums "Juggernaut: Alpha" and "Juggernaut: Omega" (2015), "Periphery III: Select Difficulty" (2016), "Periphery IV: Hail Stan" (2019), and "Periphery V: Djent Is Not a Genre" (2023). Each release showcased their growth and experimentation within the progressive metal genre.
